However, there are still some films out there that do a great job of representing Asian characters. Here are a couple examples of how Asians should be represented in film.
Minari - Free of stereotypes, Minari perfectly portrays the story of Korean immigrants moving to America in search of the American dream. By showcasing the normal life of asain immigrants, the film makes their story more relatable, showing the audience that everyone, regardless of race, goes through similar struggles.
Everything, Everywhere, All At Once - Everything, Everywhere, All at Once goes beyond the norm for an Asain film and it shows that Asian characters can be much more than the stereotypical nerd. This film showcases characters from multiple generations and the clash of ideals between each generation. It not only shows properly represents the family dynamic of an Asian household, but it also includes proper LGBTQ representation.
Crazy Rich Asians - Crazy Rich Asians is a film that does a great job at showcasing the vivid culture that Asian countries have to offer. The film follows the story of Rachel Chu as she is thrown into Singapore to visit her boyfriend's family and experience life in a different country.
